Family Emergency Checklist

The one page your family can open before they start guessing.

1. Who to call first

  • Primary emergency contact
  • Backup family contact
  • Neighbor or person with a key
  • Doctor, attorney, employer, or care manager

2. Doctors and medications

  • Primary doctor and specialists
  • Current medications and doses
  • Allergies and medical devices
  • Preferred pharmacy

3. Insurance and IDs

  • Health insurance or Medicare cards
  • Policy numbers and phone numbers
  • Photo ID location
  • Where copies are stored

4. Bills and accounts

  • Mortgage, rent, utilities, phone
  • Important due dates
  • Subscriptions to pause or cancel
  • Where payment information is kept

5. Documents and keys

  • Will, trust, POA, advance directive
  • Birth certificate, passport, car title
  • Safe, lockbox, spare key, garage code hint
  • Attorney or document contact

6. Home, pets, and wishes

  • Pet food, vet, routines, and caregivers
  • Home shutoffs, alarm, plants, vehicles
  • Care preferences your family should know
  • One message you do not want left unsaid

Tonight's conversation starter

"If you had to go to the hospital tonight, what would you want me to know first? I do not want us guessing in a hard moment."
