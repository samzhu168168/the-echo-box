Reference boundaries

The Echo Box is built as a self-reflection and privacy-first writing tool. It does not provide therapy, crisis care, legal advice, medical advice, or domestic violence support.

When we discuss safety, privacy, or emergency boundaries, we keep the claim simple and direct: if someone may be unsafe, a website timer is not enough.

Reference areas used for editorial review

Privacy by designLocal-first storage, minimal analytics fields, and no message-body tracking.
Digital safetyShared-device risk, local export/delete controls, and avoiding sensitive credentials.
Relationship safety boundariesClear redirection when a situation involves threat, stalking, violence, or self-harm risk.
Consumer clarityPlain pricing, one-time purchase language, and no subscription confusion.

Need urgent help?

If there is immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a trusted person near you. The Echo Box cannot assess danger or intervene.
