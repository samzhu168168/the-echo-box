What to include

The core sections of a family emergency binder.

Start with what people need in the first hour, then add the details that prevent weeks of confusion.

Emergency contacts Primary contact, backup contact, neighbor, doctor, attorney, employer, care manager, and who to call first.
Medical information Current medications, doses, allergies, pharmacy, primary doctor, specialists, devices, and appointment notes.
Insurance and documents Health insurance, Medicare, photo ID, will, trust, POA, advance directive, passport, car title, and where papers are stored.
Bills and home details Mortgage or rent, utilities, phone, care costs, subscriptions, keys, alarms, shutoffs, vehicles, and household routines.
Pets and dependents Pet food, vet, routines, caregivers, plants, neighbors, children, partner, and anyone else who depends on the household.
Wishes and family notes Care preferences, funeral notes, practical wishes, family messages, and reassurance. This is not a legal directive.

Who this is for

Built for the people who would have to handle everything.

The Echo Box is for adult children of aging parents, long-distance caregivers, spouses, sandwich-generation families, and anyone preparing for medical emergencies, storm season, travel, or a hard phone call.

The point is not perfect paperwork. The point is giving the right person one calm place to look.

Questions

Family emergency binder FAQ

What is a family emergency binder?

A family emergency binder is a printable file with the practical information a spouse, adult child, caregiver, or trusted person may need during a crisis: contacts, doctors, medications, insurance, documents, bills, pets, and first-call instructions.

What should I include in a family emergency binder?

Include emergency contacts, current medications, allergies, doctors, pharmacy, insurance details, ID locations, important document locations, bills, subscriptions, home instructions, pet care, and care preferences.

Why use a printable PDF instead of an app?

A printable PDF can be found and opened without a password, phone battery, account login, or internet connection. It is useful when several family members may need the same information fast.

Is The Echo Box a legal document?

No. The Echo Box is an organization tool and printable planning aid. It is not legal, medical, financial, or estate-planning advice.

For official emergency preparedness guidance, review resources from Ready.gov and the American Red Cross.
